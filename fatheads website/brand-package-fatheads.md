# Step 2B Brand Package — Fat Head's Brewery

**Prospect:** Fat Head's Brewery
**Website:** https://fatheads.com/
**Date:** April 5, 2026

---

## 1. Brand Snapshot

- **Business name:** Fat Head's Brewery
- **Overall style:** Bold, dark, craft-forward — premium craft brewery with a fun, accessible personality. The brand balances serious beer craftsmanship with an irreverent, laid-back attitude. Not pretentious — more "your cool friend who happens to make world-class beer."
- **Visual tone:** Dark backgrounds with vibrant pops of color from beer can artwork. Industrial-meets-fun. The beer packaging is colorful and illustrative while the site framework is dark and bold.
- **Tone of voice:** Casual, fun, confident, slightly irreverent. Uses phrases like "Chill out man, have a beer!" and "We're Hardcore About Brewing and Not Much Else." Avoids corporate speak. Proud but not boastful — lets the awards speak for themselves.

## 3. Color Palette

- **Primary color:** Black / Very dark (#000000 or near-black) — dominant background color
- **Secondary color:** White (#FFFFFF) — primary text on dark backgrounds
- **Accent color:** Gold/amber tones — associated with beer/brewing, used in highlights and CTAs
- **Background colors:** Black (primary), dark gray (section variation), white (occasional contrast sections)
- **Utility colors:** Each beer has its own color identity through can art (Head Hunter = green/gold, Bumble Berry = purple/blue, etc.)
- **Theme color (meta):** #000000

### Brand color DNA:
The color system is intentionally dark and bold — it creates a brewery/taproom atmosphere digitally. The vibrant beer can artwork provides the color pops against the dark canvas. This contrast is core to the brand feel and should be preserved.

---

## 4. Font / Style Direction

- **Heading font or style:** Bold, condensed, uppercase-heavy — strong brewery/industrial feel. Custom or branded heading treatment.
- **Body font or style:** Clean, readable sans-serif for body text. White on dark backgrounds with adequate contrast.
- **Button style:** Likely pill or rounded-corner buttons with bold text. CTAs use contrasting colors against dark backgrounds.
- **Border radius:** Moderate — not sharp corners, not fully rounded. Approachable but not soft.
- **Shadow / depth style:** Minimal shadow use — relies on color contrast and layering rather than drop shadows
- **Spacing feel:** Generous vertical spacing between sections. Homepage has a vertical scroll rhythm with distinct section blocks. Content breathes.

---

## 5. Layout & UI Notes

- **Header style:** Fixed/sticky dark header with logo left, main nav center/right. Includes utility menu (Beer Finder, Careers, Contact) above or alongside main nav. Hamburger menu on mobile.
- **Navigation style:** Mega-menu dropdowns with featured beer image in the Beer submenu. Clean category structure: Beer > Visit > About Us > Outreach > Shop. Location-specific subnavigation within location pages.
- **Section rhythm:** Hero → Featured Beer carousel → Promotional carousel → Visit/Locations grid → Brewing story → Crew/team → Beer Finder CTA → Events → Instagram feed → Newsletter signup → Footer. Each section is a full-width block with distinct visual treatment.
- **Card / module style:** Location cards with image banners, live hours display, and visit CTAs. Beer cards with can imagery, beer name, style label, and tagline. Event cards with date, location badge, and title.
- **Mobile feel:** Full-width stacked sections. Carousels swipeable. Navigation collapses to hamburger. Content-heavy but scrollable.

## 6. Brand DNA Summary

### What should be preserved:
- Dark, bold visual identity (black backgrounds with vibrant beer art pops)
- "Chill out man, have a beer!" attitude and irreverent copy tone
- Beer-forward hierarchy — the beer IS the brand
- Floating beer can visual treatment (specifically requested by user)
- Individual beer can art as major visual assets
- The balance between craft seriousness and fun accessibility
- Community involvement messaging
- "Fat Head's" character personality

### What can be modernized:
- Page load performance and efficiency
- Carousel-heavy layout → more intentional content hierarchy
- Award/trust signal prominence
- CTA clarity and conversion flow
- Mobile experience optimization
- Structured data and technical SEO foundation
- Location presentation and local search signals

### What should not be copied literally:
- The age gate implementation approach (the current version blocks SEO)
- The multiple competing carousels (reduce to 1-2 max)
- The buried award placement
- The excessive homepage length
- The lack of clear CTA hierarchy
- The viewport zoom restriction

### Redesign guardrails:
- DO preserve the dark color palette — this IS Fat Head's
- DO keep the beer can floating visual — it's signature
- DO maintain the fun, casual copy tone
- DO keep the Beer > Visit > About nav structure
- DON'T make it look like a generic brewery template
- DON'T remove the personality in favor of "clean minimalism"
- DON'T use light/white as the primary background
- DON'T make the awards subtle — make them proud
- DON'T lose the connection between the beer art and the site design
