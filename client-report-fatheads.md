## 1. Business Snapshot

**Business:** Fat Head's Brewery — an award-winning craft brewery with locations across Ohio and Pennsylvania, anchored by the Middleburg Heights Brewery, Beer Hall & Restaurant.

**What you do:** You brew some of the best beer in the country (GABF Brewery of the Year proves that), serve it alongside great food in a 250-seat beer hall, and distribute across 30+ states. You also host events, run community partnerships with the Guardians and Monsters, and operate a growing merchandise store.

**Why your website matters:** Your website is the front door for everyone looking up Fat Head's — whether that's a local deciding where to eat tonight, a beer fan searching for Head Hunter near them, or someone out of town checking if the Beer Hall is open. When your site makes a strong first impression, more of those people become visitors, customers, and fans.

## 2. Current Site Issues

We reviewed your current website at fatheads.com across three areas: how search engines see it, how fast it loads, and how effectively it moves visitors toward action.

Here are the most significant findings:

**Your homepage title may not be showing correctly to Google.** When search engines visit your site, they appear to see your age verification screen rather than your actual homepage content. This means Google may be indexing "Age Verification" as your page title instead of showing your business name, location, and what you're known for.

**Your biggest achievement is hidden in a rotating banner.** Being named GABF Brewery of the Year is a massive credibility builder — but on your current site, that information rotates through a carousel alongside other promotions. Most visitors will never see it. That credential deserves a permanent, prominent spot.

**Search engines are missing important business details.** Your site has only the most basic structured data (breadcrumbs). It's missing the technical markup that tells Google you're a brewery, a restaurant, where you're located, what your hours are, and what you serve. Competitors who have this markup get richer, more detailed listings in search results.

**There's no clear "do this next" for visitors.** The homepage does a great job showcasing your beers and personality, but there's no obvious primary action. Should I visit the Beer Hall? Find your beer at a store? Look at the menu? The path forward isn't clear, especially on mobile.

---

## 3. Issue-by-Issue Comparison

### Issue 1: How Google Sees Your Homepage

**Current Site:** The age gate renders before your real content, which means Google's crawler likely sees "Age Verification - Fat Heads Brewery" as the page title. This weakens your search presence for people looking for craft breweries or beer halls in your area.

**Our Version:** The page title clearly reads "Fat Head's Brewery | Award-Winning Craft Beer & Beer Hall | Middleburg Heights, OH" — giving Google the business name, category, awards, and location in one clear signal. The age gate sits on top of the content without replacing it.

### Issue 2: Awards and Trust Signals

**Current Site:** GABF Brewery of the Year appears as one slide in a rotating carousel with 5 other promotions. A visitor has roughly a 20% chance of seeing it during a typical visit.

**Our Version:** The award is prominently featured as a persistent badge in the hero section and gets its own dedicated Awards section. Every visitor sees "GABF Brewery of the Year 2025" — 35+ medals — WBC Gold. It's impossible to miss.

### Issue 3: Search Engine Business Details (Structured Data)

**Current Site:** Only basic breadcrumb markup detected. No brewery, restaurant, location, menu, or event markup. Google doesn't have a clear machine-readable picture of your business.

**Our Version:** Full LocalBusiness and Organization structured data built in — including your business type, address, hours, cuisine type, price range, and social profiles. This gives Google the detailed signals it needs to show richer search listings.

### Issue 4: Clear Visitor Action Path

**Current Site:** The homepage has multiple carousels and sections competing for attention. No single CTA dominates. On mobile, the path to finding hours, directions, or a menu requires scrolling and tapping.

**Our Version:** Clear CTA hierarchy: explore the beer lineup → visit the Beer Hall (with address, hours, and directions immediately visible) → find our beer near you. The most important action is obvious within seconds.

### Issue 5: Beer Showcase Experience

**Current Site:** Featured beers appear in a carousel — you see one at a time and must click through. The visual effect of the floating cans is cool but could work harder.

**Our Version:** We preserved the floating beer can aesthetic you love but paired it with a full beer grid that shows all your flagship beers at once — each with its own color identity, style, and personality. The effect is more immediate and more impressive.

## 4. Why the New Version Performs Better

**Clearer first impression.** Within 5 seconds, a visitor knows: this is a brewery in Middleburg Heights, they won Brewery of the Year, and the beer looks incredible. The current site needs 15-20 seconds of scrolling (after the age gate) to deliver that same picture.

**Easier navigation.** The structure is clean — Beer, Visit, About, Shop — and the most important details (hours, location, what to expect) are visible without hunting.

**Stronger local visibility foundation.** The new version gives Google the title, description, and structured data it needs to understand Fat Head's as a brewery and beer hall in Middleburg Heights. The current version may be confusing Google with the age verification screen.

**Stronger trust and professionalism.** GABF Brewery of the Year is front and center. 35+ medals. World Beer Cup Gold. Community partnerships. These aren't buried — they're the first thing that builds credibility.

**Clearer action path.** Every section guides the visitor toward the next logical step. See the beer → want to try it → here's where we are → come visit (or find us near you).
